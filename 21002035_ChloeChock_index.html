RELS 1013 Survey of World Religions
Ms Sunita
This course is an introduction to prominent World Religions: Hinduism, Buddhism, Taoism, Confucianism, Judaism, Christianity and Islam. Minor religions such as Jainism, Sikhism, Shinto, Bahai’ and Zoroastrianism are also discussed. Each tradition is explored in terms of historical development (key figures, events, evolution, etc.) and fundamental worldview (divine reality, human nature, the problem and solution for human beings, ethical teachings, life beyond death, mystical approaches, etc.). Through the course, students develop knowledge and appreciation of each religion as well as religiosity and spirituality in general. Contemporary issues, such as religion in modern life and religious pluralism, will also be explored.

COMM 1023 Introduction to Human Communication
Mr Saqib
This is an introductory course designed to provide students with a foundational understanding of human relationships. Various topics will be covered from theoretical, practical, and experiential perspectives. Emphasis will be placed on the students’ ability to articulate their understanding of the material in verbal, written, and small group contexts. Ultimately, this course is designed to facilitate the integration of complex theoretical aspects of interpersonal communication in practical ways, while stimulating students’ ability to critically think through such issues.

ENGL1014 Fundamentals of College Writing
Ms Joanna
This subject introduces students to basic skills of writing that will be beneficial in further education and the working world. Through several structured, unstructured writing and critical thinking techniques, the student engages in the task of using writing for self-expression as well as communication.

UNIL 1011 University Life – Freshman Seminar
Dr Sim
Freshman Seminar helps students to transition from secondary school to their new role and place in the university. MUSC 1033 American Popular Music
Ms Jaime Yap
This subject introduces students to popular music throughout the history of the United States of America, with emphasis on Tin Pan Alley, jazz, country music, rock and other recent styles. We will also cover the composers, lyricists, performers and other persons whose work as well as creativity, have contributed to the vast body of popular music today. You will be required to either sing or play a musical instrument for this class.

MU1 2143 Islamic and Asian Civilization Studies (Only for Malaysian student)

Semester 3

MUSC 1024 Music Theory and Musicianship I
Mr Charles Tan
This is a foundational subject that covers the fundamentals of music theory, connected to the development of basic ear training and keyboard skills. The theoretical elements include rhythm, tempo, pitch, melody, key signatures, scales, intervals, triads, chords, and harmony, and the recognition and basic analysis of musical structures through active listening. The musicianship component will be applied via singing, written dictation, and the acquisition of basic keyboard skills.

MUSC 1034 Audio Recording and Production
Prof. George
This subject introduces the fundamentals of recording and music production and explores the techniques, repertoire and aesthetics of creating music in the project studio. Students will learn to use digital audio workstation software, software synthesisers, samplers, digital signal processors, mixers, drum machines and sequencers while learning the principles and techniques of electronic and popular music. At the conclusion of the course, students will be able to compose, record and produce music on computers and in the recording studio and discuss the process using appropriate terminology.

MUSC 1054 Applied Contemporary Musicianship I
Mr Yusran Yusoff
Students will develop skills as a soloist or ensemble. The student will learn how to function in an ensemble, recognise roles, apply listening skills, develop instrumental proficiency and improvisation skills, and strive for a cohesive group sound. Notation skills including transcribing, music reading and interpreting charts, will be encouraged. Students will apply all of the above skills in preparing and presenting the final performance.

HPEL 1013 Health and Wellness
Ms Cheah Pei Yi
The course will focus on personal health from a holistic perspective. In depth attention will be given to the relationship between the body, mind, spirit, environment and community as well as to the interaction between these dimensions and the resulting impact on health and well -being. Particular focus will be given to research on the physical and mental health benefits. There is also a physical activity or physical education component to this class. This course will be team -taught by Biology, Psychology and Sports Psychology lecturers.
